01

research

During my Masters I learnt how to approach different problems and effectively research to get a sense with which kind of problem I'm dealing. At the beginning of a project I research and collect as much information as possible to brainstorm and to find out what the best solutions are. Throughout the project I define hypotheses which I then validate or disprove. Following this, I solve the problem step by step.

02

empathy

I don't believe that hard skills are the only skills needed to truly excel and help others. Therefore I always try to change my perspective and understand the customers, users and students I'm working with. By doing this, I hope to deliver exactly the solution they need and that helps them right now.

03

simplification

A key skill one learns studying physics is to break problems down to its core. To make them as simple and as explainable as possible. In data science projects, this helps me define the right problems and goals. In LX projects it helps me make the learning topic understandable. Using simplifications I also try to start with the simplest solution possible and iterate from there. "Everything should be made as simple as possible, but not simpler" - Albert Einstein.
